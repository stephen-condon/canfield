# Canfield Solitaire Rules

### Objective
The goal of Canfield is to move all 52 cards into the four foundation piles. 

### The Layout
Canfield uses a standard 52-card deck. The initial layout consists of four main areas:
1. **The Reserve (The "Demon"):** 13 cards are dealt face down into a single pile, and the top card is then turned face up. This is your reserve pile.
2. **The Foundations:** One card is dealt face up above the tableau. This card determines the "base rank" for all four foundation piles. For example, if a 7 of Hearts is dealt here, all four foundation piles must begin with a 7.
3. **The Tableau:** Four cards are dealt face up in a row to the right of the reserve. These form the start of your four tableau columns.
4. **The Stock and Waste:** The remaining 34 cards form the stockpile, which are kept face down.

### Building Rules
* **Foundations:** You must build the four foundation piles **up by suit**. Because the foundation can start on any card rank, building is continuous and wraps around from King to Ace (e.g., if the base rank is 7, a pile would build: 7, 8, 9, 10, J, Q, K, A, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6).
* **Tableau:** You build the tableau columns **down in alternating colors** (red on black, black on red). Like the foundations, building here is also continuous and wraps from Ace to King (e.g., a Black King can be placed on a Red Ace).
* **Moving Cards:** You can move single cards or fully packed sequences of cards between the tableau columns.

### Gameplay
* **Playing Cards:** You can play the top card of the Reserve, the top card of the Waste pile, or the exposed cards at the bottom of any Tableau column. These cards can be moved to the Foundations or onto other Tableau columns according to the building rules.
* **Using the Stock:** You turn cards from the Stock pile into the Waste pile. Traditionally, cards are turned over three at a time, though some variations allow turning one card at a time. You can cycle through the Stock pile as many times as necessary.
* **Empty Columns:** This is a crucial rule—if a column in the tableau becomes empty, it **must** be filled immediately by the top card of the Reserve pile. 
* **Depleted Reserve:** If the Reserve pile is completely emptied and a tableau column becomes empty, you can fill that empty column with any available card or sequence from the waste or other tableau columns.

### Winning
You win the game when all 52 cards have been successfully moved to the four foundation piles. 

*Note: Canfield is known for being a notoriously difficult solitaire game to win, requiring careful planning to ensure you don't trap necessary cards in the tableau or waste pile.*
